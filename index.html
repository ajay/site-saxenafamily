भजन बिना चैन न आए राम
Bhajan Bina Chain Na Aaye Ram
A traditional Ram bhajan emphasizing that without devotional singing, one cannot find peace. Often sung by Anup Jalota and other classical bhajan singers. The song urges listeners to devote themselves to chanting God's name, as life without bhajan is like a lotus without water.

Hindi

भजन बिना चैन न आए राम
भजन बिना चैन न आए
तन में राम, मन में राम, राम बिना सब सूना
बिन भजन के ये जीवन है, जैसे कमल बिना पानी
माया जाल पसारा जग में, मोह ने है ललचाया
जनम मरण के भवसागर में, बिन भजन डूब जाए
संत जनों का कहना मानो, हरि चरणों में ध्यान लगाओ
एक दिन सबको जाना होगा, सत्य वचन मन में बिठाओ

Transliteration

Translation

Refrain

Bhajan bina chain na aaye Ram

Bhajan bina chain na aaye

Refrain

Without devotional singing, there is no peace, O Ram,

Without bhajan, there is no peace.

Tan mein Ram, man mein Ram, Ram bina sab soona

Bin bhajan ke ye jeevan hai, jaise kamal bina paani

Ram in the body, Ram in the mind — without Ram everything is empty.

Life without bhajan is like a lotus without water.

Maya jaal pasaara jag mein, moh ne hai lalchaaya

Janam maran ke bhavsaagar mein, bin bhajan doob jaaye

Maya (illusion) has spread its web in the world, attachment has enticed us.

In the ocean of birth and death, without bhajan one will drown.

Sant janon ka kehna maano, Hari charanon mein dhyan lagao

Ek din sabko jaana hoga, satya vachan man mein bithao

Heed the words of the saints, focus your mind on Hari's feet.

One day everyone must depart — engrave this truth in your heart.

चिट्ठी न कोई संदेश
Chitthi Na Koi Sandesh
From the Hindi film "Dushman" (1998). Lyrics by Anand Bakshi, music by Uttam Singh. Most iconically performed by Jagjit Singh, with a version also by Lata Mangeshkar. While a film song about separation, it has become a deeply spiritual piece widely sung at memorial gatherings and prayer meetings — expressing the soul's longing for the departed.

गायत्री मन्त्र
Gayatri Mantra
One of the most sacred mantras in Hinduism, from the Rigveda (3.62.10). Attributed to Rishi Vishvamitra, it addresses the solar deity Savitri and is a prayer for intellectual and spiritual illumination. Often called the "Mother of the Vedas." Traditionally recited at dawn, noon, and dusk during sandhyavandana.

Sanskrit

ॐ भूर्भुवः स्वः
तत्सवितुर्वरेण्यं
भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि
धियो यो नः प्रचोदयात्॥

Transliteration

Translation

Om Bhur Bhuvah Svah

Tat Savitur Varenyam

Bhargo Devasya Dhimahi

Dhiyo Yo Nah Prachodayat

Om — the sacred sound — across the earth, atmosphere, and heavens,

we meditate upon the most adorable

radiance of the Divine Creator (Savitri).

May that divine light illuminate and inspire our intellects.

मैली चादर ओढ़ के कैसे द्वार तेरे आऊँ
Maili Chadar Odh Ke Kaise Dwar Tere Aaun
One of the most famous bhajans attributed to Sant Kabir Das (15th century poet-saint). The "chadar" (sheet) is a metaphor: the body/life given by God is like a clean white sheet, which we soil through sin, worldly attachments, and impure deeds. Most famous rendition by Hari Om Sharan. Often sung at prayer gatherings and satsangs.

Hindi

मैली चादर ओढ़ के कैसे द्वार तेरे आऊँ
मैली चादर ओढ़ के कैसे द्वार तेरे आऊँ
जब मैंने चादर ली तेरे नाम की,
ओढ़ के मैंने मैली करी
काम क्रोध मद लोभ ने धोबिया,
इसने धोबी धोई नहीं
धूप में छाँव में मैली हो गई,
धूल में मिट्टी में मैली हो गई
बिन साबुन बिन पानी के,
ये चादर धुल न पाई
कहत कबीर सुनो भाई साधो,
राम नाम रस भर दो
भक्ति प्रेम के साबुन से,
ये चादर धो के आऊँ

Translation

Refrain

Having soiled this sheet, how can I come to Your door?

When I received the sheet in Your name,

Says Kabir, listen O holy ones,

fill it with the essence of Ram's name.

With the soap of devotion and love,

let me wash this sheet and come to You.

राम नाम अति मीठा है कोई गाके देख ले
Ram Naam Ati Meetha Hai Koi Gaake Dekh Le
A beloved Ram bhajan encouraging listeners to try chanting Ram's name to experience its sweetness firsthand. Most famously performed by Anup Jalota. The bhajan draws on stories from Hindu scripture — Shabari offering berries to Ram, the story of Ajamil, and the story of Ganika — to illustrate the transformative power of God's name.

Hindi

राम नाम अति मीठा है कोई गा के देख ले
आ जाते हैं राम कोई बुला के देख ले
अँधियारा है मन का, दीपक जला के देख ले
अहंकार की गठरी, सिर से उतार के देख ले
अमोलक ये मानव तन, किसी से पूछ ले
राम नाम के बदले में, कुछ तो बेच के देख ले
शबरी के जूठे बेर, प्रभु ने खाए थे
प्रेम भक्ति की डोर से, रघुवर बँधे आए थे
अजामिल गणिका तारे, जो शरण में आए
राम भरोसे छोड़ दे, कोई छोड़ के देख ले

Transliteration

Translation

Refrain

Ram naam ati meetha hai koi gaa ke dekh le

Aa jaate hain Ram koi bula ke dekh le

Refrain

The name of Ram is very sweet — try singing it and see!

Ram comes to you — try calling Him and see!

Andhiyaara hai man ka, deepak jala ke dekh le

Ahankaar ki gathri, sir se utaar ke dekh le

There is darkness in the mind — try lighting a lamp and see.

The bundle of ego on your head — try putting it down and see.

Amolak ye maanav tan, kisi se poochh le

Ram naam ke badle mein, kuchh to bech ke dekh le

This human body is priceless — ask someone and find out.

In exchange for Ram's name, try giving something up and see.

Shabari ke joothe ber, Prabhu ne khaaye the

Prem bhakti ki dor se, Raghuvar bandhe aaye the

Shabari's half-eaten berries, the Lord ate with love.

Bound by the thread of loving devotion, Raghuvar (Ram) came to her.

Ajaamil Ganika taare, jo sharan mein aaye

Ram bharose chhod de, koi chhod ke dekh le

He redeemed Ajamil and Ganika — those who took His shelter.

Surrender everything trusting in Ram — try letting go and see.

शान्ति पाठ
Shanti Path
Two of the most commonly recited Vedic peace invocations. "Om Sahana Vavatu" (from the Taittiriya/Katha Upanishad) is a joint prayer by teacher and student for harmony and fruitful learning. "Om Dyauh Shanti" (from Yajurveda 36:17) is a universal peace prayer invoking peace across all dimensions of creation. "Shantih" is repeated three times for peace in the divine, physical, and spiritual realms.

Sanskrit — Om Sahana Vavatu

ॐ सह नाववतु ।
सह नौ भुनक्तु ।
सह वीर्यं करवावहै ।
तेजस्विनावधीतमस्तु
मा विद्विषावहै ।
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥

Transliteration

Translation

Om Saha Naavavatu

Saha Nau Bhunaktu

Saha Viryam Karavaavahai

Tejasvi Naavadhitamastu

Ma Vidvishavahai

Om Shantih Shantih Shantih

Om. May God protect us both together.

May God nourish us both together.

May we work conjointly with great energy.

May our study be brilliant and effective.

May we not quarrel with each other.

Om, peace, peace, peace.

वनस्पतयः शान्तिर्विश्वेदेवाः शान्तिर्ब्रह्म शान्तिः
सर्वं शान्तिः शान्तिरेव शान्तिः
सा मा शान्तिरेधि ।
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥

Transliteration

Om Dyauh Shantir Antarikṣam Shantih

Prithivi Shantir Apah Shantir Oshadhayah Shantih

Om. May there be peace in heaven and in the atmosphere.

श्री कृष्ण गोविन्द हरे मुरारी
Shri Krishna Govind Hare Murari
A beloved Krishna bhakti folk bhajan, famously rendered by Jagjit Singh. It covers the key aspects of Krishna's life: his miraculous birth in a prison, childhood in Gokul, relationship with Radha, and the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita. The closing includes the celebrated "Tvameva Mata" shloka. Widely sung during Krishna Janmashtami and devotional gatherings.

सीताराम सीताराम सीताराम कहिए
Sitaram Sitaram Sitaram Kahiye
A deeply popular Ram bhajan from the devotional folk tradition. The most famous recorded version features music by Ravindra Jain, sung by Anuradha Paudwal. The core teaching is surrender to God's will: "However God keeps you, remain that way." Commonly sung at satsangs and Ram katha events across North India.

Hindi

क्या गरीबी क्या अमीरी, राम जो दे सो सही
जग से तू कुछ ना माँगे, हरि कृपा से कुछ नहीं
काम क्रोध मद लोभ में, फँसे रहे क्यों दिन रात
हो गई मानव जनम की, व्यर्थ ये सारी बात
धन जोबन का घमंड न कर, ये तो चार दिन का मेला है
कल तक तेरे थे न होंगे, समझ ले तू अकेला है

Do not be proud of wealth and youth — this is but a fair of four days.

What was yours yesterday will not remain tomorrow; understand, you are alone.
